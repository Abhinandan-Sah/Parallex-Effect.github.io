Adventure Time!

Being adventurous is similar to being courageous. Stepping out for the activities that involve risk and danger develops an extraordinary quality in a human. Being adventurous means, having a strong desire to do something out of comfort. It can be adventure travel or adventure financial decisions.

There are various videogames; novels, stories, etc are based on adventure. Great novelist J.K. Rowling’s creation ‘Harry Potter’ is the perfect movie for adventure seekers. Famous Discovery Channel Show ‘Man vs. Wild’ depicts the courageous acts of Bear Grylls. Mountaineers risk their life for reaching the peak of the highest mountains around the world. Motor-Biking on hilly roads are also one of the adventurous act. Ladakh is famous for its motor-biking.

BIKING

Biking has various health benefits for both children and adults. It can reduce the risk of heart disease, stroke, cancer, type-2 diabetes, hypertension, and adiposity. It can also increase cardiovascular fitness, muscle strength, flexibility, and bone health.

Biking helps burn calories, increase muscle mass, and regulate blood pressure. Biking is a form of active transportation that improves general health. Cycling tends to be more formally used by journalists, while biking is more informal. Cycling is a competitive sport that requires a bicycle to be used and has been going on for over a century with innovations in clothing, equipment, and technique to make it a very competitive sport.

PARAGLIDING

Paragliding is an adventure sport that involves flying through the air using a paraglider. A paraglider is a lightweight, free-flying glider aircraft that is launched by running off a hill or mountain. Paragliding is a popular sport around the world and is enjoyed by people of all ages. It is a great way to experience the thrill of flying and to see the world from a different perspective. Paragliding can be done solo or in tandem with an experienced pilot .

Paragliding is a recreational and competitive adventure sport of flying paragliders. Paragliders are lightweight, free-flying, foot-launched glider aircraft with no rigid primary structure. The pilot sits in a harness or lies prone in a cocoon-like ‘pod’ suspended below a fabric wing. Despite not using an engine, paraglider flights can last many hours and cover many hundreds of kilometres, though flights of one to five hours and covering some tens of kilometres are more the norm.
